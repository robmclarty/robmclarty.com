Co-op Cabs

Online taxi ordering system that connects directly to Co-op's computerized dispatch system + public marketing website with custom integrated CMS.

Launch Website

In order to offer its customers the most convenient options for ordering a taxi, Co-op needed a responsive web application that worked on multiple platforms. Developed in Rails with heavy use of Javascript we developed custom-integrated CMS, blog, rating, and messaging systems, along with a responsive, multi-device, online cab ordering system. Customers are able to quickly fill out a simple form, geolocate themselves (or enter their desired pickup location manually), and place an order directly to Co-op's computer-controlled dispatch system. Once an order is placed, real-time status updates are provided to customers so they know where their assigned cab is on a map relative to their pickup location, eventually notifying them when it's time to go outside and hop in.

Philosophy Factory

Web design and development studio portfolio for lead generation.

Launch Website

I co-founded Philosophy Factory Inc. in 2006 and helped various clients (big and small) realize their online potential over the course of 7 years. I learned to design beautiful interfaces, how to develop custom-made web app solutions in Ruby on Rails, how to educate the uninitiated, and all the administration and management that goes into running a small business. This website was our portfolio. We wanted to emphasize the quality of our work, so we designed the homepage to jump directly into the portfolio. The main menu was a progression of the natural steps our potential clients might take leading from examples of work to wet their appetites, to explanatory details about who we were and how we worked, to ultimately conclude on our desired website goal of making contact with us. We tried to lay out the interface in as straight-forward and intuitive a manner as possible while all the time making it as quick and easy as possible to execute our desired action with links to contact us through the website or by alternative means like email, phone, or post.

Rob McLarty Blog

Responsive personal blog with simple navigation (click on article then click on logo to go back to list).

Launch Website

I put creating a blog for so long as I was wrapped up in making everyone else's website! But I neglected to foster my online presence and interact, as an individual, with the rest of net, which I felt was hindering both myself and my audience. I have built up a wealth of programming know-how, business processes, and design methodologies which have helped me achieve my own goals, and I wanted to a way to share some of that and give back to the the internet (which had provided me with so much knowledge without asking for anything in return). My goal with the design of the interface was simply "keep it simple stupid". There is no menu; only a list of links which point to individual posts and a logo/banner which points back to the list. That's it.

Verold Inc

Interactive 3D for everyone. Verold Studio is a powerful online visual editor that lets you turn 3D content into interactive presentations on the web.

Demo Videos   (web platform is no longer active)

Leveraging many bleeding-edge technologies, Verold enables users to transform their 3D content into online interactive presentations, apps, showcases, and more. Based on WebGL, the Verold embed has enabled artists, educators, marketers, and business people to better connect to their audiences through greater immersion and interaction on any device with an internet connection.

Essentially what's happening is this:
  1. bring some 3D stuff (any format)
  2. turn it into Verold™ 3D stuff (processed behind the scenes when you upload your 3D stuff)
  3. compose a scene out a library of Verold™ 3D stuff (your own or shared by the community)
  4. share it with the world using Verold's embed widget
  5. or integrate it with your web app directly using Verold's API
  6. enjoy amazing interactive 3D content rendered in the highest quality possible with WebGL using Verold's custom 3D rendering engine (based on Three.js)
